Hot Springs National Park

The park is adjacent to the city. After it was federally protected it became a very popular tourist attraction. There are 26 miles of hiking as well as scenic drives and overlooks to enjoy. The Mountain Tower is a prominent icon of the city and from the top you can view an area of 30 to 40 miles. There are also many places to enjoy fishing or bird watching.

Price: Free

Age: All ages

Garvan Woodland Gardens

Garvan Woodland Gardens is the botanical garden of the University of Arkansas. The gardens are on a peninsula off the shore of Lake Hamilton. It is most famous for its Garden of the Pine Wind Japanese Garden and the treehouse pictured above.It also includes a waterfall, canopy bridge, and a beautiful chapel.

Price: $15 for adults, $5 for children

Age: All ages

Bathhouse Row

Bathhouse Row, located inside Hot Springs National Park,is a collection of eight historic bathhouses.This is where you can actually soak in the hot springs and indulge in spa treatments. They each have their own charms. Fordyce, the most expensive and elegant, is now the visitor center. Buckstaff has been operating continuously for over a century. Hale's sauna is a thermal cave carved into the mountainside.

Price: $38-$94

Age: 10+
